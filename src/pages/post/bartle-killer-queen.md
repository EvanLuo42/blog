## Introduction

It seems to be rare to find a game that everyone loves, but at least game designers are trying their best to create games that fit the majority's taste. "Killer Queen" is one of the arcade games that has done this job remarkably well. No formal research has been done, but at least in our small classroom, almost everyone who played "Killer Queen" gave it a thumbs up, and many players go to play it immediately during breaks. Based on Bartle's taxonomy of player types, I personally think that the magic of "Killer Queen" comes from its thoughtful care for each type of player.

## Killers

The fastest way to end a set is to kill the opposing party’s queen three times. I think the warrior and the queen most fit the player type of Killer. Bartle suggests that these players want to kill other player characters to prove they are stronger than others. This is especially important for an arcade game where players clearly know which character is whom. Showing off their sophisticated skills to other players becomes more significant in the real world. 

For queen players, this perfectly fits their need to defeat others, as their main mission each turn is to protect themselves and defeat the opposing queen, making it a 1v1 game between two queens, with some involvement from warriors. 

For warrior players, this is also true, as they defeat opposing queens and warriors, and if they perform well, they may receive even more praise than the queen by successfully protecting the “incompetent” queen. They relish this kind of recognition.

## Achievers

The role of the Drone in the game best represents Achievers. As someone who identifies 53% as an Achiever, collecting things is one of my favorite activities in a game. As Bartle wrote, “Players give themselves game-related goals and vigorously set out to achieve them. This usually means accumulating and disposing of large quantities of high-value treasure, or cutting a swathe through hordes of mobiles.” With more and more berries being collected, Achievers receive continuous stimuli encouraging them to be quick. It is a great experience to see the honeycomb fill up step by step. Achievers enjoy the final achievement, but the process leading to it is equally important.

## Explorer

Although "Killer Queen" does not have a big map, as a 2D platformer-like control, Explorers may try to find the most efficient routes to run back and forth between berries and the honeycomb. They take pleasure in mastering the game's mechanics and discovering optimal strategies to enhance their performance.

## Socialisers

The game inherently encourages teamwork and communication, which is a key attraction for Socializers. In "Killer Queen," effective communication and coordination between team members are essential for success. Socializers can take on roles that involve supporting their teammates, such as planning strategies, coordinating attacks, or helping others to collect berries. The necessity of teamwork allows Socializers to engage deeply with their team, fostering a sense of camaraderie and collaboration.

## Conclusion

"Killer Queen"’s mechanics and characters perfectly fit every player type described by Bartle’s taxonomy, and this is the reason why it is so attractive to a wide range of players.
